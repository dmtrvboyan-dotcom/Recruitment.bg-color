# Automate Your Hiring Pipeline with Smart Workflows

Hiring is one of the most time-intensive processes in any growing company. Recruiters spend hours manually moving candidates between stages, sending follow-up emails, and coordinating with hiring managers. Smart workflow automation changes all of that.

## What Are Smart Workflows?

Smart workflows are rule-based automation sequences that trigger actions based on candidate activity or recruiter decisions. Think of them as your silent recruiting assistant — always on, never forgetful.

Common examples include:
- **Auto-advance** a candidate to a phone screen when their resume score exceeds a threshold
- **Send a rejection email** 24 hours after a candidate has been in "Under Review" with no activity
- **Notify the hiring manager** instantly when a candidate completes their technical assessment

## The 40% Time-to-Hire Reduction — How It Happens

Our customers who fully adopt workflow automation report cutting their time-to-hire by an average of **40%**. The gains come from three places:

### 1. Eliminating Manual Stage Transitions
Each manual stage move takes 2–5 minutes. Multiply that across 50 candidates and 6 stages, and you're looking at hours of work every week. Automation handles this instantly.

### 2. Faster Candidate Communication
Candidates who receive timely updates are 3× more likely to stay engaged. Automated emails sent within minutes of a decision keep your pipeline warm without lifting a finger.

### 3. Reduced Coordination Overhead
Instead of Slack pings and status meetings, hiring managers get real-time notifications exactly when they need them.

## Getting Started

Setting up your first workflow takes less than 10 minutes:

1. Navigate to **Settings → Workflows**
2. Choose a trigger (e.g., "Candidate submits application")
3. Add one or more actions (e.g., "Send confirmation email", "Score resume with AI")
4. Set any conditions (e.g., "Only if position is Engineering")
5. Activate and let it run

## Pro Tips

- Start with your highest-volume stage — usually the initial application review
- Use the **workflow analytics dashboard** to measure time saved per stage
- Combine AI scoring with auto-advance for a fully hands-off top-of-funnel

Automation doesn't replace recruiters — it frees them to focus on what matters most: building real relationships with great candidates.
